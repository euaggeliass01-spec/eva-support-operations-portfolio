Case Study: Research Outreach Operations System

Evidence type: Self-directed operations improvement
Scale: 30+ research contacts, six targeted CV variants

Problem

A broad search across universities and research groups created a coordination problem: different research areas required different CV positioning, contacts could overlap, automatic replies created follow-up dates, and duplicate outreach would look careless.

Solution

I built a structured tracking system with:

  • institution, city, lab, contact, role, and email;
  • research category and matching CV version;
  • sent status and date;
  • reply classification;
  • follow-up date and next action;
  • priority;
  • duplicate and overlap notes.

Impact

  • Centralized more than 30 outreach targets.
  • Managed six role-specific CV variants.
  • Reduced the risk of duplicate messages and missed follow-ups.
  • Made it possible to review the whole pipeline by status and next action rather than relying on memory.
  • Created a reusable process for future academic or job outreach.

Relevance to Automattic

This is a small example of going beyond the immediate task. Instead of only sending messages, I created an operating system for the work. The same instinct applies to support: identify repeated friction, organize the information, and make the next action easier for everyone.
